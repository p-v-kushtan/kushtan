the product :

The application let user create playlists with ability to edit duration of each song in the playlist using own music library.

the problem :

Workouts are often interval. The classic solution for switching from one exercise to another is timer setting. It takes time, concentration and not comfortable during training.

the goal :

Create an application that will use music to solve the problem of tracking intervals: the user'll be able to set the time and amount of intervals with simple playlist creation.

my role :

UX designer designing an app from conception to delivery: creation of design system, paper and digital wireframing, low and high-fidelity prototyping, conducting usability studies, accounting for accessibility and iterating on designs.
okey, it's time for the first step: empathizing

user research : summary

The main conclusion of the study: people involved in sports - are the main audience. But other target users were also identified: students, cooks and people who need multiple iterating intervals of time in their work.

user research : pain points

alert :
Users feel frustrated when they need to watch on watches or phone screen to know the time during workout. Usually they solve it with timer alarm but it interrupts music and also brings negative effect;
process :
The process of setting timers or alarms for intervals is long and inconvenient.

persona : meet yanina

Yanina is a coach and an athlete - she visits the gym every day and wants training to be a moment of relaxation for her.

user journey map

Yanina’s user journey shows the importance of work-process without the need for constant control by the user, as well as the simplicity and intuitiveness of the interface.

Goal: complete interval training.

usability study : findings

  1. Users feel more comfortable when step skip option is highlighted;
  2. Users wants to control the end of each action.

more mockups

The blue color wasn’t chosen accidently. The choice was inspired by the so-called ‘The blue steak experiment’, which declared the instinctive level of human perception of blue color in food. Despite the two opposing camps that have been created on this issue, the researchers agree with the primitive human instinct that encourages us to perceive blue food as uneatable, since in the natural environment suitable for food products of this color are rare.

The amount of dark blue color in the background varies depending on the step of flow (light - getting started - until it is ready (dark blue).

Gestalt principal 'Similarity': buttons, widgets and titles of the current operation are made with inner shadow.

accessibility considerations

  1. Color system based on contrast ratio (4.06-16.24);
  2. Control is easy for both: right and left-handed users;
  3. Elements with common function have similar view through design.
