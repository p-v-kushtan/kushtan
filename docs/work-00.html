the product :

We’re creating an app to provide guests of an art-gallery easy and comfortable way to get an excursion. As a rule - personal guide excursions are expensive, hard-coordinated and don’t provide enough language support.

the problem :

Personal guide excursions at art-galleries are expensive, hard-coordinated and don’t provide enough language support.

the goal :

Design audio-tour guide app which let galleries guests to get an excursions regardless of their ability or native language.

my role :

UX designer designing an app from conception to delivery: conducting interviews, paper and digital wireframing, low and high-fidelity prototyping, conducting usability studies, accounting for accessibility and iterating on designs.
okey, problem's defined, let's start empathize with the user

user research : summary

After conducting a series of interviews and analyzing the data obtained, the following group was identified: art-gallery visitors - local and tourists: from 12 years old to the elderly, who want to quickly and easily get an excursion in their own language.

As the group is broad, was make a decision to take users' common pain points, basing on accessibility. Specifically: possibility to listen and read excursion wholly, and also to have access to information about art-objects from any point of gallery.

user research : pain points

language :
Not all tourists speak English good enough to expereience excursion comfortable;
informational architecture :
There are elderly users with no digital literacy;
audio and visual support(a11y) :
There are users with different disabilities – all of them must have an opportunity to use app;
reliability of information :
Some users as artists and art connoisseurs need verified and in-depth information about art-objects.

persona : meet gerard

Gerard is a retiree travel-lover who needs to feel comfortable using technology in museums and art-galleries during his travels because technology are often confusing but without them museums experience is incomplete.

usability study : findings

The second round of research revealed new points in improving the accessibility of design.

round 1 findings :
  1. Users need miltiple language choice;
  2. Users wants to use excursion in several ways (audio, textual);
  3. Users require optional in-depth Information about art-objects.
round 2 findings :
  1. Users need to adjust exhibitions list for choosing one;
  2. Users want to go to homepage quickly after exhibition’s finished;
  3. Users with low digital literacy needs more explicit click areas.

accessibility considerations

  1. Provided multiple support of excursion expereince: audial and visual;
  2. Easy access to tutorial and FAQ section for users with low digital literacy;
  3. Icons are easy to interpret in navigation and through all design, menu sections are screen reader friendly.
