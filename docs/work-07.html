The project:

To create an art space for the adrtist Ditto where they can create their art and share it.

Introduction

What is art?

People say this is what makes life beautiful and also you will never have to change batteries or remember passwords or read directions or fear obsolescence.

People are used to going to museums, theaters, cinemas, galleries for art. But in our time of digital technologies and pandemics, places of art are changing and becoming more accessible. Museums such as MET, Louvre, Guggenheim have opened their doors to all people of the world, making it possible to visit their galleries online using VR technologies.

The artist's space is also undergoing changes: and if the physical-tangible space used to be a platform for creativity, now the online space is also becoming one. Yes, you can't smell the paint in it, but you can be with art t√™te-√†-t√™te.

Understanding the user

Project challenge:

I needed to create a design that would simultaneously meet the requirements of the artist and their target audience. It was necessary to create a solution that would become both a space of creation and a direct exhibition hall. It will have to reflect the creativity of the project and at the same time have already intuitive familiar elements of web design.

To begin with, I needed to conduct research, determine the target audience and make a statement of the problem and the goal of the project.

After conducting an interview and analyzing the data, I identified the main target audience groups:

  • art lovers
  • art-galleries bookers
  • designers and people who need paintings for commercial and personal purposes

Pain points:

  • The artist's creative space is usually closed to the eyes of most people
  • It's hard to find conceptual artists
  • Authentic authors are often only available in galleries and private exhibitions

Goal statement:

The digital art space will be able to help users gain access to conceptual art and the artist's works, which will allow them to enjoy art from anywhere, get acquainted with the author and order their works.

Starting the design

To begin with, I needed to determine the main user path, so I created a user flow and sitemap, based on which I made sketches of the main pages and their functionality.

I have divided the main areas of the author's work into sections:

  • The basic collection of paintings
  • Prose and poetry
  • Conceptual projects

(As the content scales, it will be possible to divide the main collection into subsections, but so far this has not been necessary.)

By adding the homepage and contact pages, I built the client's main path and the site map, based on them I made the first sketches:

Wireframing

After ideation and paper wireframes I created low-fidelity prototype.

The main structure for layout: splitting the screen interface in half. In the mobile version ‚Äì horizontally, in the desktop version ‚Äì vertically. This will give a lot of negative space and air, will allow to make accents and also it adds creative impression.

After conducting the usability study, I made a refining based on the findings:
  1. The user is more comfortable when there is additional navigation in the footer (the artist was categorically against footers (why?because) but I managed to convince him of his need);
  2. Hide the characteristics of the pictures: in the mobile version, they make the interface heavy.

UI, Prototying and building

Based on lo-fi, I started to develop the design concept of the project. I needed to take photos of the artist's paintings and prepare photo and text content for the product. Working with the text involved creating the voice of the product: in this case, it was friendly and philosophical.

The colors:

Definitely, the focus should be on the author's work, so I took only black and white palette.

The typography:

For typography, I chose Miriam Libre for text and Headland One for headlines ‚Äì they combine perfectly and create the impression of a single font, despite the different families. MiriamLibre sans serif is perfectly readable in texts, and headland one looks clean and expensive in headlines with medium and large size.

Functional elements and states:

For buttons and links, I made a simple design with underlining and changing background and font colors.
