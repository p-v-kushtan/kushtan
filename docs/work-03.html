the product :

A platform for structured language learning for people who want to study by own.

the problem :

Classical courses with an academic structure often imply a choice between individual expensive training or group training with a more affordable price range. Those who are more comfortable doing it on their own have to spend a lot of time developing a program, but for this you need to know the language.

the goal :

Design a platform with created program and resources for study language by own.

my role :

Designing a product from conception to delivery: creation of concept, conductiong and analysing researches data, develipong UI Kit, implementing usability stud findings, checking sequence of brand identity in each element.
okey, it's time for the first step: empathizing

user research : summary

Users want to customize their training by time, money and the presence of a teacher.

user research : pain points

process :
Classes in a group or with a teacher are uncomfortable;
time :
The time for studying appears unexpectedly but courses require a clear schedule;
finances :
Personal teachers’ lessons are expensive.

persona : meet andrey

Andrey gives a lot of time to work and wants to spend the rest selectively.

now it's time to start the design (oh, yes)

paper wireframes

During sketching process mobile flow was defined first. Design based on future responsive behaviour.

digital wireframes

Wireframes based on a ‘Progressive enhancement ‘ approach (reason of the choice: users’ pain point - time). According to the approach, the navigation view of the menu and footer expands in the transition to the desktop version of the site.

The main elements are arranged according to the width of the viewport.

low-fidelity prototype

Two flows were created (mobile and desktop): pages ‘home’ and ‘about’ made for product presentation and service descriptions. Pages ‘theory’ and ‘dosed’ as the main resources for studying. Interaction across the entire site has intuitive elements: a hamburger menu for the mobile version, a basic carousel for an array with videos and call-to-actions buttons. All visual clutter was reduced.

Both prototypes have a single structure only with a change in navigation.

accessibility considerations

  1. Mobile version users have the same informational experience as desktop users;
  2. Design is simple and clear: clutter’s reduced;
  3. Buttons have wide tap-area;
  4. Colors with low brightness rate have decorative function only.
